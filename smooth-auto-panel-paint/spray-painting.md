### Signs Your Vehicle Paint Needs Restoring
New Zealand’s harsh climate, road stone chips, and supermarket car park scrapes degrade your vehicle’s clear coat and expose underlying steel. Common issues we resolve daily include:
*   **UV Clear Coat Peeling (Sun Damage):** Faded patches and white peeling layers occur when solar UV rays break down the top clear coat.
*   **Bare-Metal Stone Chips:** Road grit chips expose under-coat primer and bare metal, causing localized steel oxidation and rust.
*   **Mismatched Panel Pigments:** Poor third-party repairs leave doors, guards, or bonnets showing noticeably different paint shades under natural sunlight.
*   **Bumper Scrapes and Gouges:** Plastic bumper scrapes ruin vehicle curb appeal and require plastic welding, priming, and baking.

### Caring for Your New Baked Paint Finish
*   **Avoid Automated Car Washes:** Do not take your vehicle to high-pressure automated car washes for the first 30 days to let the clear coat cure.
*   **Wash with Neutral Soap:** Hand wash with cool fresh water and pH-balanced automotive shampoo. Avoid household dish detergents.
*   **Dry with Microfiber Towels:** Use clean, high-absorption microfiber drying cloths. Avoid harsh synthetic sponges or squeegees.
