### Common Areas and Causes of Vehicle Rust Decay
Vehicle rust is caused by constant exposure to road moisture, trapped mud in wheel arches, and scraped panel steel. Structural corrosion is the primary reason cars fail their Warrant of Fitness (WOF) in New Zealand. Common warning signs include:
*   **Paint Bubbling Around Trims:** Small rust pockets swelling beneath the paint surface on doors, guards, or windscreen channels.
*   **Failed WOF Structural Rust:** Deep pitting or rust holes on underbody sills, chassis rails, or floor pans flagged by WOF inspectors.
*   **Rusted Windscreen Channels:** Trapped water beneath rubber window seals causing hidden sills to decay, leading to wet cabin carpets.
*   **Corroded Seat Belt Mounts:** Structural steel erosion near seat belt anchoring brackets or suspension mounts that compromises safety.

### Frequently Asked Questions About Certified Rust Repairs
*   **Q: Can a car pass its WOF with cosmetic body filler over rust?**
    *   *A:* No. Under NZTA safety regulations, cosmetic body filler cannot be used to cover structural rust. Active rust must be cut out and replaced with welded steel plate.
*   **Q: How do WOF inspectors check underbody rust?**
    *   *A:* Inspectors use visual probes and hammers to tap chassis rails, sills, and floor pans. Flaking metal or rust holes will result in an immediate WOF failure.
*   **Q: Can you repair windscreen channel rust?**
    *   *A:* Yes. We remove the glass, cut away rusted metal from the channel, weld new steel strips, paint with anti-rust primers, and reinstall the windscreen.
*   **Q: Do you provide a structural weld compliance certificate?**
    *   *A:* Yes. We provide an MTA-backed invoice and repair report detailing the cut-and-weld execution, which validates compliant repairs for WOF recheck inspectors.
