### Common Scenarios
## Why Rust Causes Immediate WOF Failures
Vehicle rust is caused by road moisture, trapped mud in wheel arches, and paint scratches. In New Zealand, rust is a primary reason cars fail their Warrant of Fitness (WOF) checks. We treat and repair these common areas:
*   **Paint Bubbles:** Small rust pockets swelling beneath the paint on door edges, guards, or windscreens.
*   **Underbody Sill Rust:** Deep rust or holes on the chassis rails or floor pans flagged by WOF inspectors.
*   **Windscreen Frame Rust:** Trapped water beneath window seals causing hidden metal to rust, leading to cabin leaks.
*   **Corroded Seat Belt Mounts:** Rust near seat belt anchoring brackets or suspension mounts that compromises safety.

### Aftercare Guide
## Preventing Future Rust on Restored Panels
Proper aftercare and regular maintenance help reduce moisture buildup, slow corrosion, and protect newly repaired metal surfaces from future rust development.
*   **Clean Underbody Wheel Arches:** Regularly spray out your wheel arches to clear trapped mud and road salts.
*   **Repair Paint Stone Chips Promptly:** Touch up paint chips quickly to prevent moisture from rusting bare steel.
*   **Park Under Cover When Possible:** Parking inside a garage or carport keeps metal dry and slows steel oxidation.

### Frequently Asked Questions
## Essential Questions About Structural Rust and Inspections
Here are answers to some of the most common questions regarding structural rust repairs, WOF failures, certified welding, underbody corrosion, and compliance requirements.
*   **Q: Can a car pass its WOF with cosmetic body filler over rust?**
    *   *A:* No. Under NZTA safety regulations, cosmetic body filler cannot be used to cover structural rust. Active rust must be cut out and replaced with welded steel plate.
*   **Q: How do WOF inspectors check underbody rust?**
    *   *A:* Inspectors use visual probes and hammers to tap chassis rails, sills, and floor pans. Flaking metal or rust holes will result in an immediate WOF failure.
*   **Q: Can you repair windscreen channel rust?**
    *   *A:* Yes. We remove the glass, cut away rusted metal from the channel, weld new steel strips, paint with anti-rust primers, and reinstall the windscreen.
*   **Q: Do you provide a structural weld compliance certificate?**
    *   *A:* Yes. We provide an MTA-backed invoice and repair report detailing the cut-and-weld execution, which validates compliant repairs for WOF recheck inspectors.
*   **Q: How long does a certified sill rust repair take?**
    *   *A:* Standard sill rust cut-and-weld repairs typically take 1 to 2 business days, depending on trim disassembly, weld coordinates, and paint matching.
