### Signs Your Vehicle Needs Structural Panel Repairs
Even minor impacts or hidden rust can severely compromise your vehicle’s structural integrity and Warrant of Fitness legality. Common signs your vehicle requires professional panel beating include:
*   **Crumpled Guard or Door Panels:** Crushed sheet metal blocks door operations and ruins your vehicle’s visual lines.
*   **Uneven Panel Gaps:** Misaligned gaps between doors, hoods, or boots indicate underlying structural shifts after a collision.
*   **Failed WOF Structural Rust:** Rust bubbles on structural pillars, sills, or underbody joints require urgent NZTA-compliant welding.
*   **Heavy Commercial Frame Twisting:** Heavy trucks or vans showing uneven tyre wear or pulling after accidents require frame alignment.

### Traditional Panel Beating vs. Paintless Dent Removal (PDR)
Choosing the right dent repair method depends on your vehicle’s paint condition:
*   **Paintless Dent Removal (PDR):** Best for minor supermarket parking dings where the paint is unbroken. We use specialized rods behind the panel to push the dent out without repainting, saving you time and cost.
*   **Traditional Panel Beating:** Required when collision damage has cracked the paint or creased the sheet metal. We pull the metal back, apply smoothing primers, and perform computerized paint refinishing.

### Caring for Your Restored Body Panels
*   **Avoid Waxing Fresh Paint:** Do not apply heavy waxes or polishes to fresh paintwork for the first 30 days to let the clear coat cure.
*   **Gentle Hand Wash Only:** Hand wash with fresh water and pH-neutral car wash soap. Avoid automated brush washes.
*   **Keep Clear of High Sun Exposure:** Park your vehicle in shaded areas or garages where possible to protect new paint from high UV rays.
