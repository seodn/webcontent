### Caring for Newly Painted Car Parts
*   **Handle Replacement Panels Gently:** Avoid commercial high-pressure jet washes near new panel edges for 14 days.

### Frequently Asked Questions About Painting Car Parts
*   **Q: Can you match the color of my car if the paint has faded?**
    *   *A:* Yes. We do not just mix paint by factory code. We scan your car’s actual panel with a digital spectrometer to match any sun-fading, ensuring a seamless blend.
*   **Q: Do I need to leave my entire car at the shop if I only need a part painted?**
    *   *A:* Not necessarily. If you bring us the replacement component and a small removable part (like your tow eye cover or fuel door) for color matching, you can keep driving your car.
*   **Q: Can you paint raw plastic aftermarket bumpers?**
    *   *A:* Yes. Raw plastic requires specialized plastic adhesion promoters and flexible primers to prevent the paint from cracking or flaking off during parking bumps. We apply all necessary primers.
*   **Q: Do you paint custom body kits and side skirts?**
    *   *A:* Yes. We paint aftermarket fiberglass, carbon fiber, and polyurethane spoilers, side skirts, valances, and body styling kits.
*   **Q: How long does it take to paint a single bumper or door?**
    *   *A:* A single car part typically takes 24 to 48 hours to complete prep, priming, spectrometer painting, baking, curing, and polishing.
