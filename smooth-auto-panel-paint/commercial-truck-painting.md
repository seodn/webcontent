### Common Scenarios
## Everyday Wear and Tear That Damages Commercial Vehicles
Commercial fleets and industrial trucks face road gravel, loading scrapes, and chemical exposures that damage paint and lead to rust. We fix these common issues daily:
*   **Cargo Area Scrapes:** Forklifts and heavy cargo scratch truck bodies, exposing bare steel that rusts quickly.
*   **Chemical & Fuel Spills:** Diesel and hydraulic leaks eat away at chassis paint, leading to corrosion.
*   **Chassis Stone Chips:** Road gravel chips away at underbody paint, resulting in failed structural WOF checks.
*   **Faded Branding & Decals:** Sun-faded paint and peeling company decals look unprofessional and lower your fleet's value.

### Aftercare Guide
## Caring for Heavy Vehicle Paintwork
Proper aftercare helps protect your commercial vehicle’s paintwork from fading, chemical exposure, and premature wear. Following these simple maintenance steps can extend the lifespan of your refinished surfaces.
*   **Allow Paint to Cure:** Avoid harsh chemical washes for the first 14 days following heat-baking.
*   **Clean Fuel Spills Promptly:** Rinsing off raw fuel or hydraulic spills keeps your paint looking vibrant.
*   **Hand Wash Fleet Cabs:** Wash fleet cabs with soft brushes or mitts rather than aggressive automated industrial truck washes.
