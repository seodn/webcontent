### Common Causes of Commercial Paint and Corrosion Damage
Commercial transport fleets and industrial trucks face severe daily road grit, chemical exposures, and heavy loading scrapes that expose metal panels to rust. Common structural paint issues we resolve daily include:
*   **Cargo Panel Abrasion & Scrapes:** Forklifts and heavy cargo scratch truck box interiors and tailgates, leaving bare metal exposed to rust.
*   **Chemical & Fuel Corrosion:** Diesel spills, hydraulic fluid leaks, and road salts eat away at chassis paint, causing structural steel decay.
*   **Chipped Chassis Under-Coats:** Heavy road gravel chips chassis paint, leading to deep under-body corrosion that fails structural WOFs.
*   **Faded Corporate Fleet Branding:** Sun-faded paintwork and peeling commercial decals damage your brand image and lower fleet resale value.

### Choosing Between Polyurethane Paint and Standard Auto Finishes
Heavy transport operators typically weigh polyurethane against standard acrylic paints:
*   **Industrial Polyurethane Paint:** Highly recommended for heavy commercial trucks, box vans, and trailers. It cures to a rock-hard finish that resists diesel spills, road gravel impacts, and forklift abrasions.
*   **Standard Acrylic Urethane Finishes:** Best for standard company passenger cars and executive fleet vehicles where a mirror-like high-gloss finish is prioritized over absolute physical durability.

---

### Caring for Heavy Vehicle Polyurethane Paintwork
*   **Allow Coating to Cure Fully:** Avoid harsh chemical cleaners and heavy solvent washes for the first 14 days following heat-baking.
*   **Clean Diesel Spills Promptly:** While polyurethane is chemical-resistant, rinsing off raw fuel or hydraulic spills keeps paint vibrant.
*   **Hand Wash Fleet Cabs:** Wash fleet cabs with soft brushes or mitts rather than aggressive automated industrial truck washes.
