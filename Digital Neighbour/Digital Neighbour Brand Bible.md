# THE STORY OF SAGAR BHOLA

Digital Neighbour did not start in 2022.

The company may have been registered in 2022, but the journey started much earlier.

The journey started in 2013.

While studying at university in India, Sagar developed a strong interest in marketing, websites, SEO, and the digital world.

Unlike many people who discovered digital marketing after entering the workforce, Sagar became interested in it while still studying.

During this period he completed marketing and digital marketing internships, gaining exposure to how businesses attract customers and how websites, search engines, and online marketing work together.

One of the most valuable experiences during this period was gaining exposure to websites and website back-end systems.

This helped him understand:

- How websites function
- How businesses communicate online
- How customers find businesses
- How search engines discover websites
- How online visibility influences business growth

This exposure also introduced him to SEO and search rankings.

Even during those early years, he began understanding concepts such as:

- Target audience
- Target market
- Customer behaviour
- Search intent
- Online visibility

While many people see websites as digital brochures, Sagar began understanding them as business tools.

That understanding would later become one of the foundations of Digital Neighbour.

# MOVING TO NEW ZEALAND

In 2017, Sagar moved to New Zealand.

This created a completely new chapter.

Rather than immediately pursuing entrepreneurship, he focused on learning.

He completed further study in New Zealand, including a Graduate Diploma in Applied Management.

During this period he continued developing his understanding of business and marketing.

The marketing modules were particularly influential because they expanded his perspective beyond local business marketing.

He began understanding:

- International business
- International marketing
- Business strategy
- Product positioning
- Market segmentation
- Customer journeys

These studies reinforced an important lesson.

Marketing is not about selling.

Marketing is about understanding people.

# UNDERSTANDING THE NEW ZEALAND MARKET

One of the most important experiences in Sagar’s journey was working in retail.

Many people underestimate retail experience.

For Sagar, retail provided a practical education in New Zealand consumer behaviour.

It helped him understand:

- How New Zealand customers think
- How purchasing decisions are made
- What influences trust
- What influences loyalty
- What customers value

This experience helped bridge the gap between academic theory and real-world business behaviour.

Understanding people became just as important as understanding marketing.

# THE DIGITAL MARKETING INDUSTRY EXPERIENCE

After completing his studies, Sagar joined a digital marketing company and spent approximately four years working within the industry.

This experience became one of the most important chapters in shaping Digital Neighbour.

Working inside a digital marketing agency exposed him to:

- Small businesses
- Medium-sized businesses
- Enterprise organisations
- Business owners
- Marketing managers
- Sales teams
- Operational teams

This period provided a deep understanding of how businesses operate across New Zealand.

More importantly, it provided insight into how agencies operate.

Sagar observed what worked.

He also observed what did not work.

One recurring pattern stood out.

Many business owners were investing significant money into marketing services while having very little understanding of what they were actually purchasing.

Many did not understand:

- Domains
- Hosting
- Websites
- SEO
- Google Ads
- Analytics
- Rankings

In many situations, services were being sold before clients fully understood whether those services were even appropriate for their goals.

This created a growing belief that there was an opportunity to do things differently.

# WHY DIGITAL NEIGHBOUR WAS CREATED

Digital Neighbour was never created simply to sell digital marketing.

It was created to provide honest advice.

One of the strongest beliefs that influenced the company was simple.

Not every business needs the same service.

For example:

A business needing immediate enquiries may benefit from Google Ads.

A business wanting long-term visibility may benefit from SEO.

A business with a poor website may need a website before investing heavily in marketing.

The recommendation should always depend on what the client needs.

Not on what the agency wants to sell.

This philosophy became one of the foundations of Digital Neighbour.

The goal was to create a business where:

- Education comes first
- Advice comes first
- Client goals come first
- Long-term growth comes first

The sale comes second.

# STARTING DIGITAL NEIGHBOUR

Digital Neighbour was founded by Sagar Bhola in Christchurch in August 2022.

At the beginning, there was no large team.

There was no large office.

There was no massive agency structure.

The business started with a simple belief.

Build something meaningful.

Help businesses.

Deliver quality.

If it works, keep growing.

If it does not work, learn from it and move forward.

The early stage involved handling almost everything personally.

This included:

- Sales
- Discovery meetings
- Websites
- Hosting
- SEO
- Google Ads
- Social Media
- Customer service
- Project management

The company was built client by client.

Project by project.

Relationship by relationship.

One of the key beliefs from day one was that talent is not restricted by geography.

The right person for the role could be anywhere.

This philosophy eventually led to building a team across multiple locations while maintaining New Zealand leadership and direction.

# THE CORE BELIEF

If there is one belief that defines Digital Neighbour, it is this:

We would rather recommend the right service than sell the wrong service.

Everything else comes from that belief.
