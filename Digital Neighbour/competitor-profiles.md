# Competitor Profiling Report: Digital Neighbour & The NZ Search Market

*Last updated: June 2026*

This document provides a comprehensive competitive landscape analysis of Digital Neighbour's core competitors in New Zealand (and globally where relevant). By evaluating each competitor's positioning, GTM strategies, strengths, weaknesses, and service structures, Digital Neighbour can reinforce its positioning as the top 1% Search Agency in New Zealand, bridging traditional SEO/Ads with the conversational AI search engines (Gemini, ChatGPT, Siri).

---

## 1. Executive Summary & Market Dynamics

The New Zealand search market is in a transition phase. As search behavior shifts from traditional keyword searches on Google to conversational queries across AI interfaces (ChatGPT Search, Google Gemini, Siri/Apple Intelligence, and Perplexity), agencies are responding in one of three ways:
1.  **The Creative Generalists (e.g., The Web Guys, Totally Digital):** Focus on premium bespoke designs, photography, videography, and e-commerce builds, treating SEO and AI Search (AEO/GEO) as supplementary service items.
2.  **The Lead-Gen Performers (e.g., Do Good Things):** Focus on paid advertising and HubSpot CRM integrations with a bold "no-fluff, revenue-first" messaging model.
3.  **The Academic Technologists (e.g., ThatWare):** Global operations utilizing dense data-science terminology (NLP, vector databases) and scaling automated algorithms, but lacking local market trust or practical business-friendly language.

**Digital Neighbour's Opportunity:** Occupy the high-trust, technically elite "sweet spot"—matching the technical schema nesting and speed engineering of ThatWare, but delivering it in the plain-English, relationship-first, Christchurch/Auckland-grounded style of Sagar Bhola and Pranav Tipre.

## 2. Porter's Five Forces Scorecard

This scorecard evaluates the overall attractiveness and competitive intensity of the New Zealand digital search and growth agency industry.

| Force | Intensity (1-5) | Impact | Key Factors |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Threat of New Entrants** | 4 | High | Low capital barriers to start a basic agency. However, establishing high-trust authority, custom speed engineering, and advanced AI integration capabilities requires significant expertise, which protects the top tier. |
| **Bargaining Power of Suppliers** | 2 | Low | Wide availability of software, hosting providers, and technical platforms. Switching costs for agency tools (e.g., Ahrefs, SEMrush) are minimal. |
| **Bargaining Power of Buyers** | 4 | High | Local New Zealand SMEs have many choices and are highly price-sensitive. They demand transparency and real revenue outcomes. Lock-in contracts are increasingly disliked, forcing agencies to adopt open-term structures. |
| **Threat of Substitutes** | 3 | Medium | Internal hiring of entry-level digital marketing staff, DIY platforms (Wix, Shopify automated ads), and generic AI tools (e.g., ChatGPT for basic content writing) are substitutes. |
| **Competitive Rivalry** | 4 | High | Intense competition among local creative, performance, and SEO agencies. Many compete on generic price packages, though few can deliver advanced schema nesting and AI search readiness. |

**Overall Industry Attractiveness:** Moderate. While competition and buyer power are high, agencies that differentiate via technical excellence (AI search readiness, sub-1.5s load speeds) and client-focused business logic (open terms, transparent education) can capture premium, loyal accounts.

Digital Neighbour shifts the boundaries of traditional NZ digital marketing by applying value innovation to create an uncontested market space: **The Hybrid Search Agency**.


*   **ELIMINATE:**
    *   **Lock-in long-term contracts:** Transitioning entirely to month-to-month, outcome-driven open relationships.
    *   **Jargon-heavy reports:** Eliminating automated PDF keyword dumps that business owners do not understand.
    *   **Dashboard theatre:** Removing generic charts celebrating "impressions" or "reach" that don't correlate to real business inquiries.

    *   **Sales-first conversations:** Reducing upfront sales pressure by prioritizing auditing and educational discovery.

    *   **Client education:** Empowering business owners by explaining search changes using real-world analogies (e.g., "The Mall Analogy").
    *   **Technical schema nesting:** Building advanced JSON-LD structured schemas linking local services to professional NZ registries.
    *   **Speed engineering:** Setting a hard limit of sub-1.5s load speeds for crawler compatibility and user experience.
*   **CREATE:**
    *   **The AI Search Readiness Audit:** An interactive lead hook showing prospects how they appear on ChatGPT, Gemini, and Siri compared to local competitors.
    *   **Share of Voice tracking for AI:** Active auditing and tracking of brand recommendation metrics inside conversational LLM search outputs.

## 5. Individual Competitor Profiles

### Competitor 1: The Web Guys

*   **Website:** https://thewebguys.co.nz/
*   **Overview:** Auckland-based boutique digital agency founded to create beautiful, custom online experiences. They specialize in high-end design, custom development, and visual brand strategy.

#### Service Catalog & Tech Stack
*   **Core Offerings:** Custom website design, WordPress & WooCommerce development, custom mobile applications, photography & videography (including drone footage), branding & visual identity.
*   **SEO Capabilities:** Basic on-page SEO setups, domain management, and standard Google Analytics integrations.
*   **Ecosystem:** Webflow, WordPress, WooCommerce, custom PHP, and professional media suite tools.

#### Go-To-Market (GTM) Strategy
*   **Messaging:** Focuses on creative visual storytelling, premium craftsmanship, and "websites that work as hard as you do."
*   **Hook:** Visual case studies showcasing stunning photography, brand redesigns, and custom app interfaces.
*   **Client Target:** Mid-to-high end New Zealand retail, lifestyle brands, commercial builders, and corporate entities who value high visual aesthetics.

#### Strengths & Advantages
*   Outstanding visual design, high-quality media production assets (custom photography, video, drone footage).
*   Strong reputation in Auckland for custom application development.

#### Weaknesses & Gaps
*   SEO is treated as a component of custom website design rather than a dedicated technical growth engine.
*   Lacks advanced technical schema nesting, local directory authority syncing, and AI search engine optimization (AEO/GEO) capabilities.

#### Strategy & Likely Moves
*   Continue expanding their creative media production and custom application services.
*   Likely to partner with external SEO agencies to handle technical search campaigns while retaining creative control.

### Competitor 2: ThatWare

*   **Website:** https://thatware.co/
*   **Overview:** Global AI-first search engineering agency headquartered in India, focused on high-end enterprise technical SEO, NLP mappings, and automated search algorithms.

#### Service Catalog & Tech Stack
*   **Core Offerings:** Advanced technical SEO, AI SEO (GEO/AEO), NLP semantic search optimization, cognitive SEO, automated link building, crawl budget optimization, and semantic schema architectures.
*   **SEO Capabilities:** Specialized packages for ChatGPT, Siri, Gemini, and voice search optimization.
*   **Ecosystem:** Custom machine learning scripts, advanced schema generators, NLP text analysis APIs.

#### Go-To-Market (GTM) Strategy
*   **Messaging:** Highly technical, math-heavy, and enterprise-focused. Uses complex terminology like "cognitive SEO," "semantic algorithms," and "vector database retrieval."
*   **Hook:** High-tech audit tools, proprietary SEO tools, and case studies proving search dominance for major global brands.
*   **Client Target:** Enterprise companies, large e-commerce brands, and technical teams looking for scale.

#### Strengths & Advantages
*   Deep technical knowledge of search engine algorithms, crawling, and AI databases.
*   Low-cost global resource structure allowing massive content and backlink execution.

#### Weaknesses & Gaps
*   **Jargon Overload:** Alienates local New Zealand business owners with complex, academic explanations.
*   **Lack of Local Trust:** No physical presence in Auckland or Christchurch; unable to offer hands-on local support or face-to-face consultation.
*   Does not offer high-converting, localized web design or local business conversion engineering.

#### Strategy & Likely Moves
*   Double down on white-labeled technical SEO for large agencies.
*   Expand their proprietary AI search engine ranking tools.

## 8. Digital Neighbour's Differentiators & Counter-Strategies

Digital Neighbour structurally wins against these competitors by applying five core philosophies:

1.  **Translate Technology into Plain English:**
    *   *Competitor Gap:* ThatWare and corporate agencies use dense, complex jargon (NLP, vector databases, RAG pipelines).
    *   *DN Response:* We explain changes using practical Kiwi analogies. (e.g., *“Google is no longer the entire mall; it is now one of many stores inside the mall. ChatGPT and Siri are other stores.”*)
2.  **Strategy & Discovery Before Selling:**
    *   *Competitor Gap:* Traditional agencies push packages in their first sales pitch to meet quotas.
    *   *DN Response:* We analyze market opportunities, client services, and target audiences before proposing a customized search blueprint.
3.  **Complete, Education-First Transparency:**
    *   *Competitor Gap:* Agencies keep client dashboards intentionally confusing to hide lack of performance.
    *   *DN Response:* We educate clients so they understand *what* is happening, *why* it is happening, and *what* they are paying for.
4.  **No Lock-in Contracts (Open-Term Trust):**
    *   *Competitor Gap:* Competitors lock Kiwi businesses into 6-12 month contracts to secure revenue.
    *   *DN Response:* We prefer month-to-month agreements. We believe trust should be earned and maintained daily. If a client no longer sees value, they are free to leave.
5.  **Technical SEO-First Custom Development:**
    *   *Competitor Gap:* Creative agencies (The Web Guys) build pretty sites with poor search setups. Digital agencies (Totally Digital) use heavy builders.
    *   *DN Response:* We build custom, fast websites (sub-1.5s load speeds) with nested geographical/registries schema out of the box because *“a website is the foundation of a house; if the foundation is weak, the SEO walls will crack.”*
